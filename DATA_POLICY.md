# Data Policy

**Status:** policy for this repository. Preliminary research project; this policy
applies to all material committed here and to any run performed via local
execution or CI.

## Principle: synthetic-only

All stimuli (prompts, scenarios, experimental materials) in this repository are
**synthetic or abstract**. **No real personal data is used.**

## Prohibited — never commit or process here

- Real **personal / identifying** data (names, contact details, accounts).
- **Patient / clinical** data of any kind: medical records, diagnoses tied to a
  person, and neuro/biomedical data such as **TBI records, PET, MRI, EEG, blood,
  or genomic** data.
- Third parties' **private communications** or any confidential / unpublished
  data not cleared for public release.

These must not be placed in the repository, in prompt sets, in results, or sent
through any local run, hosted API, or CI workflow.

## Prompts and experimental materials

Synthetic or abstract only. Where a scenario resembles a real situation (e.g.
caregiving in the coexistence-resilience direction), it must be **fully
constructed/synthetic** — never derived from a real person, patient, or family.

## Model outputs (`experiments/results/`)

- Outputs are generated by models from the synthetic prompts in this repository.
- They carry full provenance (model, version, date, settings, prompts) and may be
  subject to the model/provider terms (see [`LICENSING.md`](LICENSING.md)).
- A human reviews outputs before they are committed; outputs must not contain
  real personal data.

## Cloud / CI caution

Running in GitHub Actions or any third-party service sends the inputs to that
service. Only the synthetic material in this repository may be used. **Do not**
stage confidential, unpublished, personal, or clinical data in any workflow.

## Why this matters

Keeping the program strictly synthetic protects research subjects, avoids
privacy/ethics violations, and keeps the work freely shareable — which is the
point of an open, reproducible research program.
